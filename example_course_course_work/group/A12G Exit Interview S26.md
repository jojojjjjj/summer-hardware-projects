# Purpose of the Exit Interview

The exit interview is designed to give you an opportunity to **reflect on your work** over the semester and **consolidate your understanding** of the concepts you have applied.

 This is a valuable time to critically review what you have accomplished, what you might improve, and how your technical and teamwork skills have developed.

At the same time, the discussion aims to help you **prepare for future technical interviews**, where you may be asked to explain your project work, design decisions, and problem-solving approaches in a similar format.

# Exit Interview Content Overview

## 1\. General Questions

You will be asked several general questions about your project experience, including:

* An introduction to your project and a brief explanation of how work was divided among team members.  
* A summary of what was completed and what remains unfinished.  
* If you had the opportunity to redo a part of your project, what would you choose and why?  
* How would you like to further improve your project?  
* etc.

## 2\. Technical Questions

During the exit interview, each group will also participate in a **technical discussion** focused on the design, implementation, and conceptual understanding of your project. This portion is intended to assess both **individual** and **group** comprehension of the system you have built, including your design choices, how your hardware and firmware function under various conditions, and your ability to reason about and explain key aspects of your implementation.

**Expectations:**

* Each group member is expected to meaningfully contribute to answering at least two questions.  
* Approximately **two questions per group member** will be asked, based on your hardware or firmware contributions.

**Hardware Topics:**  
 Prepare to describe the hardware elements you worked with or designed, including but not limited to:

* PCB layout basics:  
  * Power planes  
  * Separation of analog/digital sections  
* Power design considerations:  
  * Buck converters, boost converters, buck-boost converters  
  * Voltage regulation for microcontrollers and sensors  
  * Power distribution and decoupling capacitors  
* Signal integrity considerations:  
  * Differential pairs (e.g., for high-speed UART/SPI)  
  * Ground loops and grounding strategy  
  * PCB trace design for analog and digital signals  
* Sensor integration:  
  * Analog vs. digital sensors  
  * Noise filtering techniques  
  * Pull-up/pull-down resistors for I2C, UART

**Firmware/Software Topics:**  
 Prepare to discuss software concepts related to your project, including but not limited to:

* Embedded Systems Concepts:  
  * FreeRTOS basics and task management  
  * Multithreading and thread synchronization  
  * Race conditions and strategies for prevention  
  * Use of mutexes and semaphores for resource protection  
* Communication Protocols:  
  * I2C  
  * SPI  
  * UART  
* Peripheral and Device Control:  
* Timers  
* PWM  
* ADC  
* GPIO  
* Security and Data Integrity:  
  * CRC  
* Cloud Integration:  
  * MQTT

**Additional Notes:**

* You may indicate whether you prefer to answer hardware-related or software-related questions.  
* If you make a mistake or get stuck, you will be given the opportunity to try a different question within the same area.  
* Please aim to speak clearly and confidently — the goal is to demonstrate your technical reasoning and contributions.

**Reminder:**  
 Your TAs are there to support you — use this discussion to showcase the depth of your understanding and your ability to troubleshoot and think critically.
